Explore South Sikkim

A cultural and geographic sanctuary home to manicured tea estates, towering sacred statues, and ridge walks.

1. Buddha Park (Tathagata Tsal)

Located beautifully in the quiet town of Ravangla, this majestic park features a stunning 130-foot tall bronze statue of Lord Buddha consecrated directly by the Dalai Lama. Framed against alpine mountains, the site encompasses immaculate walkways, large water fountains, and a quiet temple sanctuary inside the base structure.

2. Temi Tea Garden

Established in 1969, Temi is proud to be the only active commercial tea garden in Sikkim, famous globally for its premium, organic orthodox tea leaves. The sweeping estate rolls across wave-like mountain slopes, welcoming travelers to walk through tea bushes, try fresh leaf tasting sessions, and visit old processing factories.

3. Siddhesvara Dhaam (Char Dham)

Sitting atop Solophok Hill right outside Namchi town, this cultural complex displays an impressive, towering 108-foot statue of Lord Shiva. The vast complex features detailed, scale replicas of India's four most sacred spiritual shrines (Badrinath, Jagannath, Dwarka, and Rameshwaram) surrounded by manicured garden grounds.

4. Samdruptse Hill

Known locally as the "Wish Fulfilling Hill," Samdruptse features a colossal 148-foot statue of Guru Padmasambhava (Guru Rinpoche) covered in shimmering gold and copper paint. Looking out over pristine forests, it offers serene views across nearby river valleys and holds a rich museum gallery detailing old historical photos of the state.

5. Tarey Bhir

Tarey Bhir is an incredible 10,000-foot long steep stone cliff edge featuring a long, secure concrete walkway similar to a miniature Great Wall. Walking down this path treats travelers to breathtaking vertical views directly into deep valleys, where the Teesta and Rangeet rivers snake through rural mountain ranges below.

6. Maenam Wildlife Sanctuary

Perched high above Ravangla at a peak height of 10,600 feet, this dense alpine reserve is a premier destination for wilderness trekking. The sanctuary is rich in unique flora and fauna, including medicinal mountain herbs, towering oak woods, rare red pandas, barking deer, and colorful pheasants.

☀️ Great Climate

South Sikkim is generally characterized by lower elevations and milder winter weather conditions than the North. It remains accessible to travelers all year round without requiring complex border check permissions.
