Explore East Sikkim

Discover legendary border heights, the ancient loop tracks of the Silk Route, and the charms of Gangtok.

1. Gangtok

Gangtok is the beautiful, exceptionally clean mountain capital of Sikkim. Acting as the ultimate launchpad for all your Himalayan travel circuits, this vibrant town perfectly blends deep monastic heritage with contemporary walking avenues, multi-cuisine cafes, and sweeping window lookouts over distant cloud-kissed ridges.

2. Tsongmu Lake (Changu Lake)

Positioned calmly at a high mountain pass of 12,400 feet, this sacred oval-shaped glacial lake is legendary for its shifts in appearance. Its waters match the clear blue sky in summer, shimmer beautifully during spring, and freeze over completely into thick sheets of glass during winter.

3. Nathula Pass

Peaking at an impressive 14,140 feet, this historically strategic mountain crossroad branches directly off the ancient Old Silk Route. It acts as an active border checkpoint connecting India directly with Tibet, providing staggering mountain horizons and unforgettable views of international frontier paths.

4. Zuluk

Zuluk is an extraordinarily unique hamlet famous across the globe for its mind-bending **Zig-Zag Silk Route loops**. Boasting more than 30 hairpin turns along a single cliff side, driving this dramatic mountain highway provides travelers with an unrivaled adrenaline rush and unmatched vantage viewpoints over the Eastern Himalayas.

5. Padamchen

Tucked away inside thick, pristine pine groves at a very pleasant alpine climate, Padamchen is an idyllic, peaceful village on the lower Silk Route loop. Celebrated for its dense bird populations, beautiful weather, and panoramic valley views, it offers a wonderfully soothing retreat to break up your journey before pushing up toward higher altitudes.

6. Aritar

Known for its emerald green woods and rich history, Aritar is highly famous for **Lampokhari Lake**—the oldest natural emerald boot-shaped lake in Sikkim that now offers peaceful paddle boating. Surrounded by thick green forests and historical British bungalows, it provides an offbeat charm that travelers absolutely adore.

10 Essential Sightseeing Spots Around Gangtok

Make sure your local itinerary includes these top 10 classic hotspots located right around the capital:

1. Rumtek Monastery

Sikkim's largest and most famous seat of Tibetan Buddhist architecture and art treasures.

2. Tashi Viewpoint

Offers breathtaking sunrise vistas overlooking Mt. Kanchenjunga on clear mornings.

3. Ban Jhakri Falls & Energy Park

A scenic 100-foot cascading waterfall themed perfectly around local shamanic folklore.

4. Namgyal Institute of Tibetology

A world-renowned museum holding rare old manuscripts, cloth paintings (Thangkas), and artifacts.

5. Do Drul Chorten Stupa

An iconic holy dome structure bordered neatly by 108 sacred prayer wheels.

6. Gangtok Ropeway (Cable Car)

Gives you a thrilling bird's-eye view floating high over Gangtok city and surrounding ravines.

7. Enchey Monastery

A peaceful, 200-year-old sanctuary beautifully protected by tall whispering pine groves.

8. Directorate of Handicrafts & Handlooms

A live craft center showcasing local artisans weaving authentic carpets, blankets, and woodwork.

9. Flower Exhibition Centre

A spectacular greenhouse filled to the brim with exotic Himalayan orchids and seasonal flora blooms.

10. Bakthang Waterfall

A broad, scenic roadside waterfall that makes for a perfect photo stop on your way out of town.

📄 Permit Required

Tsomgo Lake, Nathula Pass, Zuluk, and Padamchen lie inside highly sensitive border zones. A Protected Area Permit (PAP) must be arranged ahead of time.

Documents Needed:
  • Passport size photos (2 copies)
  • Valid Photo ID (Voter Card / Passport / Driving License)
  • *Note: Aadhaar cards are generally not accepted at military outposts.
