Explore East Sikkim

Discover the vibrant mountain capital, deep cultural roots, and the ancient historic Silk Route tracks.

1. Tsomgo Lake (Changu Lake)

Tsomgo Lake is a sacred glacial lake nestled quietly at an altitude of 12,400 feet. Celebrated for its oval shape and stark mountain reflections, the lake reflects completely different colors across seasons—from turquoise blue water in summer to a solid sheet of stark ice in peak winter. Visitors love enjoying traditional, colorful Yak rides right along its scenic shoreline.

Travel Tip: The weather changes rapidly here. It is highly recommended to start your journey early from Gangtok so you can comfortably explore the shores before afternoon mountain mists set in.

2. Nathula Pass

Standing tall at 14,140 feet, Nathula Pass forms a vital international gateway linking India directly to Tibet (China). Historically an offshoot track of the legendary Old Silk Route, it treats travelers to dramatic, sweeping alpine lookouts, views of heavy border security outposts, and beautifully stark, rugged mountain terrains.

Travel Tip: Nathula Pass is open exclusively to Indian nationals from Wednesday through Sunday. It remains closed to travelers on Mondays and Tuesdays for security purposes.

3. Gangtok Capital Circuit

Gangtok is the highly progressive yet serene, clean mountain capital of Sikkim. It offers a wonderful blend of rich traditional culture and dynamic city lifestyle. Key places to explore on a local sightseeing tour include the historic Rumtek Monastery, the informative Namgyal Institute of Tibetology, flower exhibitions, and the popular vehicle-free walking strip of MG Marg for local dining and shopping.

4. Baba Harbhajan Singh Mandir

Located between Nathula Pass and Jelepla Pass at a height of 13,123 feet, this touching roadside shrine honors the memory of Indian Army soldier Baba Harbhajan Singh. Troops firmly believe his guardian spirit continues to patrol these rough high borders, protecting deployed mountain regiments. It stands out as an deeply emotional and historically unique place visited by thousands of loyal citizens each month.

📄 Permit Required

Tsomgo Lake, Baba Mandir, and Nathula Pass fall entirely inside protected border territory. A Protected Area Permit (PAP) must be obtained a day in advance.

Documents Needed:
  • Passport size photos (2 copies)
  • Valid Photo ID (Voter Card / Passport / Driving License)
  • *Note: Aadhaar cards are often not accepted by border security checkpoints.
