Explore West Sikkim

A land of massive glass skywalks, sacred wishing lakes, ancient kingdom ruins, and suspension bridges.

1. Skywalk Pelling

As India's very first glass skywalk, this structural marvel stands facing a monumental 137-foot statue of Chenrezig (Avalokitesvara). Walking out across the transparent glass floor suspended high over deep mountain drops offers an exhilarating thrill alongside beautiful panoramic views of the surrounding hills.

2. Khecheopalri Lake

Deeply revered as a sacred 'wishing lake' by both Buddhists and Hindus, this peaceful body of water is neatly hidden inside dense forests. An astonishing local legend holds true to this day: leaves are instantly picked up by birds the moment they drop onto the water's surface, keeping the holy lake perfectly clean all year.

3. Rabdentse Ruins

Step back in history at the ancient archaeological site of Rabdentse, which served as the second capital city of the former Kingdom of Sikkim from 1670 to 1814. Walking through the stone palace monuments and broken columns gives you spectacular open views down into the deep valley far below.

4. Singshore Bridge

Singshore Bridge is a magnificent engineering marvel, widely recognized as one of the highest suspension footbridges across the entire Asian continent. Stretching over 198 meters long across a massive canyon gorge, standing on the middle of the bridge treats you to thrilling views of plunging waterfalls and deep green abysses.

5. Varsey Rhododendron Sanctuary

Spanning across the Singalila Range bordering Nepal, this rich nature reserve covers 104 square kilometers. During the spring months of April and May, the entire hillside sanctuary erupts into brilliant shades of crimson red and pink as rhododendron flowers burst into full bloom, making it a dream destination for wild treks.

6. Pelling Bird Sanctuary

Located amidst the lush forest reserves connecting Pelling and Rabdentse, this sanctuary is an absolute paradise for bird watchers and wildlife photographers. It is home to hundreds of rare alpine bird species, including colorful laughingthrushes, vibrant sunbirds, partridges, and winter migrating flocks nesting in the quiet green canopies.

🏔️ Accessibility

Unlike North or East regions, most prime spots around West Sikkim and Pelling do not require special restricted border permits for domestic Indian tourists, making travel plans highly flexible.
